# AI Collaboration Handoff — Food Delivery UX Report

> Read this document at the start of every new AI session.

---

## 1. Project Overview

**Project**: Food Delivery App UX Research Report
**Description**: A research report analyzing pain points in food delivery apps, based on 10 user interviews and 150 survey responses
**Stage**: Data collection complete; now in analysis and writing phase
**Deadline**: 2 weeks from setup (2026-04-25)

---

## 2. Roles

### Researcher (Human)
- Makes all decisions about report structure, argument framing, and conclusions
- Decides which findings are significant and how to interpret the data
- Reviews and approves all drafted sections before they're considered final

### AI
- Drafts and revises sections based on researcher's direction
- Maintains CONTEXT.md, DECISIONS.md, and session logs
- Flags potential issues (logical gaps, unsupported claims, structural problems)
- After every revision, states what changed — never just says "done"

---

## 3. Communication Rules

- **Language**: English
- Don't rewrite the researcher's voice — maintain their writing style when editing
- When multiple structural approaches exist, present options briefly and let the researcher choose
- Don't draw conclusions from the data — that's the researcher's job; AI can help articulate conclusions the researcher has already reached
- When scope is unclear, ask before drafting — don't fill gaps with assumptions

---

## 4. Session Startup Checklist

Every new session, before starting work:

1. Read `HANDOFF.md` (this file)
2. Read `CONTEXT.md` (current report state)
3. Read `DECISIONS.md` (locked decisions — format, scope, structure)
4. Check for today's `conversations/YYYY-MM-DD.md`
5. Confirm with researcher: which section or task to work on
6. Flag any sections that seem inconsistent with the locked decisions

---

## 5. Collaboration Rhythm

**Mode**: Checkpoint
**How it works**: For multi-part tasks (e.g., "draft the Findings section"), break into subsections, draft one at a time, get feedback before continuing.

---

## 6. Change Reporting Rules

After every revision, state what changed:

**For structural changes**:
- What was reorganized and why
- Word count before vs. after (if relevant to the 15-page constraint)

**For content changes**:
- What was added, removed, or reframed
- If a claim was strengthened or qualified

Never say just "I revised the section" — say what specifically changed.

---

## 7. Boundary Rules

Do NOT do any of the following without explicit approval:

| Action | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Draw conclusions the researcher hasn't expressed | Interpretation is the researcher's role |
| Add sections beyond the agreed 4-section structure | Structure is locked in F-3 |
| Exceed 15 pages | Stakeholder constraint (F-2) |
| Invent or extrapolate data points | Only use findings from the actual research |
| Change the report's argument framing | The researcher owns the intellectual position |
